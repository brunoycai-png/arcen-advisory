Quick Answer: The most structurally sound China entry path for European brands is three phases: Phase 1 — personal accounts for audience building with maximum flexibility and zero structural commitment (free); Phase 2 — Tmall Global for initial e-commerce revenue without a Chinese entity; Phase 3 — Chinese subsidiary (WFOE) established, all commercial operations under the owned entity. This minimises upfront investment, preserves strategic flexibility, and builds toward full market ownership.

There is no single right moment to enter China. There are right sequences. European brands that try to do everything at once — establishing a company, registering accounts, launching campaigns, and opening stores simultaneously — almost always make at least one structural mistake that costs them years to correct.

  • 1
    Phase 1: Audience Building
    Duration: 3–6 months. Structure: Personal accounts on Douyin, Xiaohongshu, and Weibo — free, requires only a mobile number and ID. No Chinese entity required. No commercial store opened. Objective: Test content formats, build a follower base that can be migrated via phone number transfer, generate organic data that informs Phase 2 decisions. Trigger to Phase 2: Consistent content engagement, growing follower base, inbound purchase interest.
  • 2
    Phase 2: Initial Monetisation
    Duration: 6–18 months. Structure: Maintain personal social media accounts + open Tmall Global as the primary e-commerce entry point. Products ship from bonded warehouses. No Chinese entity required yet. Objective: Convert social media audience into revenue, build GMV history, test product-market fit at commercial scale. Key constraint: Tmall Global cannot convert to a Chinese entity later — plan for it to be a parallel channel in Phase 3. Trigger to Phase 3: Consistent Tmall Global revenue + social media following that justifies corporate account investment + organisational readiness.
  • 3
    Phase 3: Full Market Entry
    Cost: ~USD 7,330 one-time setup + ~USD 1,070–1,790/year operating. Structure: Establish WFOE through FDI. Register all corporate social media accounts under the Chinese subsidiary. Open corporate stores. Continue Tmall Global as a parallel channel. What this achieves: Full account ownership, "V" verification badge, access to advertising tools and analytics, legally protected profit repatriation (subject to 10% withholding tax and audited financials), protection under China's Foreign Investment Law.

Cette approche minimise l'investissement initial et construit vers la pleine propriété du marché.

Il n'y a pas de moment idéal unique pour entrer en Chine. Il y a des séquences correctes. Les marques européennes qui essaient de tout faire en même temps font presque toujours au moins une erreur structurelle qui leur coûte des années à corriger.

核心结论：对欧洲品牌来说，结构上最稳健的中国进入路径分三个阶段：第一阶段——用个人账号积累粉丝，最大灵活性，零结构承诺（免费）；第二阶段——通过天猫国际获得初始电商收入，无需中国主体；第三阶段——完成 WFOE 设立，所有商业运营在自有主体名下。这种分阶段方式最小化前期投入，保持战略灵活性，逐步实现完整的市场所有权。

进入中国没有唯一正确的时机，但有正确的顺序。试图同时完成所有事情的欧洲品牌——设立公司、注册账号、发起活动、开设店铺——几乎无一例外地会犯至少一个结构性错误，需要数年才能纠正。

  • 1
    第一阶段：积累粉丝
    持续时间：3–6个月。架构：在抖音、小红书、微博注册个人账号——免费，仅需手机号和身份证件，无需中国主体。目标：测试内容格式，建立可以通过更换手机号迁移的粉丝群，生成为第二阶段决策提供依据的有机数据。进入第二阶段的触发条件：持续稳定的内容互动、不断增长的粉丝基础、主动询问购买。
  • 2
    第二阶段：初步变现
    持续时间：6–18个月。架构：保持个人社媒账号 + 以天猫国际为主要电商入口。产品从保税仓发货。暂不需要中国主体。关键约束：天猫国际事后无法转换为中国主体——规划将其作为第三阶段的平行渠道。进入第三阶段的触发条件：天猫国际收入稳定 + 社媒粉丝规模足以支撑企业账号投资 + 组织就绪。
  • 3
    第三阶段：全面进入市场
    成本：约 USD 7,330 一次性设立 + 约 USD 1,070–1,790/年运营。架构：通过 FDI 完成 WFOE 设立。在中国子公司名下注册所有企业社媒账号。开设企业店铺。天猫国际继续作为平行渠道。实现成果：完整账号所有权、"V"认证标识、广告工具和数据分析权限、依法保障的利润汇回权（须缴纳10%预提所得税并提供经审计的财务报表）、受中国外商投资法保护。

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the phased China market entry approach

Can Phase 3 be skipped entirely by operating permanently through Tmall Global and personal accounts?

Yes — and some brands choose this path for extended periods. The trade-off is permanent exposure to personal account structural risks (no V badge, limited transaction caps) and Tmall Global's structural lock-in (no conversion to Chinese entity). For brands that want full brand control, advertising capability, and profit repatriation rights, Phase 3 is not optional — it is simply a matter of timing.

Does the phase model apply to both B2C and B2B brands?

The phased approach applies primarily to B2C and lifestyle brands. B2B brands entering China typically prioritise WeChat Official Account and Baidu presence over Douyin and Xiaohongshu, and may move directly to WFOE establishment if their primary objective is contracting and invoicing rather than social media audience building.

How does this model apply to sports organisations specifically?

Sports organisations typically follow a Phase 1 strategy centred on Weibo for broadcast-style announcements and Douyin for short-form video. Phase 2 often involves merchandise e-commerce through Tmall Global. Phase 3 focuses on a Chinese entity that can enter local sponsorship and commercial partnership agreements.

Questions fréquentes

Questions courantes sur l'approche d'entrée en Chine par phases

La Phase 3 peut-elle être entièrement ignorée ?

Oui — et certaines marques choisissent cette voie pendant de longues périodes. La contrepartie est une exposition permanente aux risques structurels des comptes personnels et au verrouillage structurel de Tmall Global. Pour les marques qui veulent un contrôle total de la marque, des capacités publicitaires et des droits de rapatriement des bénéfices, la Phase 3 n'est pas optionnelle.

Le modèle de phases s'applique-t-il aux marques B2B ?

L'approche par phases s'applique principalement aux marques B2C et lifestyle. Les marques B2B entrant en Chine priorisent généralement WeChat Official Account et Baidu, et peuvent passer directement à la création d'une WFOE.

Comment ce modèle s'applique-t-il aux organisations sportives ?

Les organisations sportives suivent généralement une Phase 1 centrée sur Weibo pour les annonces et Douyin pour la vidéo courte. La Phase 2 implique souvent le merchandise via Tmall Global. La Phase 3 se concentre sur une entité chinoise pouvant conclure des accords de partenariat commercial local.

常见问题

关于分阶段中国市场进入方式的常见问题

可以完全跳过第三阶段，永久通过天猫国际和个人账号运营吗？

可以——有些品牌在较长时间内选择这条路。代价是永久暴露于个人账号的结构性风险（无"V"认证标识，交易额受限）和天猫国际的结构性锁定（无法转换为中国主体）。对于想要完整品牌控制权、广告能力和利润汇回权的品牌，第三阶段并非可选——只是时机早晚的问题。

分阶段模型适用于 B2B 品牌吗？

分阶段方式主要适用于 B2C 和生活方式品牌。进入中国的 B2B 品牌通常优先考虑微信公众号和百度存在，如果主要目标是签约和开票而非社媒粉丝积累，可能会直接进入 WFOE 设立阶段。

这个模型如何具体适用于体育组织？

进入中国的体育组织通常以第一阶段策略为中心——微博用于广播式公告，抖音用于短视频内容。体育组织的第二阶段通常涉及通过天猫国际开展周边商品电商，第三阶段专注于建立能够签署本地赞助和商业合作协议的中国实体。
