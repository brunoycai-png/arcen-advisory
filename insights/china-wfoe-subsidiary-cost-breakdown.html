Quick Answer: Setting up a WFOE in China costs approximately RMB 50,400 (~USD 7,330) as a one-time setup fee, plus RMB 3,800–5,000/year for accounting, RMB 3,600–7,300/year for annual audit, and an optional RMB 40,000/year (~USD 5,800) for a legal representative proxy. Deregistration costs approximately RMB 7,200 (~USD 1,100). All figures are 2025 estimates based on consultation with Chinese legal and accounting firms.

For European brands evaluating whether to establish a Chinese subsidiary, the question is rarely "should we do it" — the structural advantages are clear. The question is "what does it actually cost, and when does the investment make sense?"

One-Time Setup Cost

Foreign Investment Entry and Company Registration

Total: RMB 50,400 (approximately USD 7,330)

This all-in fee covers the complete establishment process:

  • Assistance with approval procedures for foreign investment entry
  • Company registration and related matters
  • Registered business address (first year)
  • Business licence issuance
  • Tax registration and account setup
  • Bank account opening

This does not include registered capital, which is a separate contribution. Under the amended PRC Company Law (effective July 2024), foreign investors must commit to a capital contribution timeline of no more than five years.

Annual Recurring Costs

These fees cover standard monthly bookkeeping, VAT filing, and corporate income tax returns. Most early-stage foreign-invested enterprises qualify as small-scale taxpayers initially. General taxpayer status is required for annual VAT taxable sales above RMB 5 million.

Annual Audit

Annual audit is a legal requirement for all Chinese companies. It is also a prerequisite for profit repatriation — no audited financials means no legally compliant dividend distribution to the overseas parent.

Optional Costs

Legal Representative Proxy

Approximately RMB 40,000/year (~USD 5,800)

Every Chinese company must have a designated legal representative — an individual whose name appears on the business licence. This role can be filled by:

  • A senior executive from the overseas parent company (recommended — eliminates this annual cost)
  • A professional proxy service provider (~RMB 40,000/year)

Recommendation: Where possible, appoint a senior executive from the overseas parent as the legal representative of the Chinese entity. This eliminates the proxy cost, ensures stronger governance control, and avoids the risks of third-party proxy arrangements.

Exit Cost

Company deregistration: RMB 7,200 (~USD 1,100) one-time

This covers the liquidation report and tax/business deregistration procedures. It does not include any outstanding tax liabilities or employment-related costs that may arise from the liquidation process.

When Does the Investment Make Sense?

The WFOE setup cost of approximately USD 7,330 one-time is the price of full platform access, full account ownership, and legally protected revenue extraction from China. For comparison:

  • A single Tmall Global annual fee is USD 5,000–10,000 — without the structural protections
  • A full-service Chinese marketing agency retainer typically runs USD 3,000–8,000/month
  • Rebuilding a Douyin account from zero after a failed operating partnership is measured in lost follower equity and months of reset

For any European brand that intends to generate commercial revenue from Chinese social media within 18–24 months, the WFOE is the only structure that provides the ownership security to justify that investment.

该一体化服务费涵盖完整的设立流程：外资准入审批、公司注册、注册地址（第一年）、营业执照、税务登记开户、银行开户。

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about WFOE setup costs and timelines

Can we start with a Chinese agent operating our accounts and migrate to WFOE later?

Technically possible for Weibo and Xiaohongshu (Chinese entity to Chinese entity transfer). Not possible for Douyin — account entity changes are not permitted. The practical recommendation is to establish the WFOE before registering any platform accounts, so all accounts go directly under your owned entity from day one.

How long does WFOE registration take?

Typically 8–16 weeks from initial filing to bank account opening, depending on city and business category. Social media account registration can begin once the business licence and bank account are in place.

Are there ongoing government fees on top of the accounting and audit costs?

Annual industry and commerce registration renewals are typically minimal (a few hundred RMB). The primary ongoing costs are the accounting, audit, and optional legal representative fees listed in this article. All fees are estimated based on consultation with Chinese legal and accounting firms and are subject to change.

Questions fréquentes

Questions courantes sur les coûts et délais de création d'une WFOE

Peut-on commencer avec un agent chinois et migrer vers une WFOE plus tard ?

Techniquement possible pour Weibo et Xiaohongshu (transfert entité chinoise vers entité chinoise). Pas possible pour Douyin — les changements d'entité de compte ne sont pas autorisés. La recommandation pratique est d'établir la WFOE avant d'enregistrer tout compte plateforme.

Combien de temps prend l'enregistrement d'une WFOE ?

Généralement 8 à 16 semaines du dépôt initial à l'ouverture du compte bancaire, selon la ville et la catégorie d'activité.

Y a-t-il des frais gouvernementaux récurrents en plus de la comptabilité et de l'audit ?

Les renouvellements annuels d'inscription au commerce et à l'industrie sont généralement minimes (quelques centaines de RMB). Les principaux coûts récurrents sont les frais de comptabilité, d'audit et de représentant légal optionnel détaillés dans cet article.

常见问题

关于 WFOE 设立成本和时间线的常见问题

我们可以先让中国代理运营账号，之后再迁移到 WFOE 吗？

微博和小红书在技术上可行（中国主体之间转让）。抖音不可行——不允许变更账号主体。实际建议是在注册任何平台账号之前先完成 WFOE 设立，让所有账号从第一天起就直接在自有主体名下。

WFOE 注册需要多长时间？

从提交申请到银行开户通常需要 8–16 周，具体取决于城市和业务类别。营业执照和银行账户到位后即可开始注册社媒账号。

除了记账和审计费用，还有其他年度政府费用吗？

年度工商年检费用通常很少（几百元人民币）。主要经常性费用是本文列出的记账、审计和可选法代费用。所有费用均为估算值，以实际咨询结果为准。
