What We Changed: The Technical Implementation

1. Complete Meta Tag Rebuild

The original <head> had a title tag, a basic meta description, and two Open Graph properties. The rebuilt <head> contains:

  • Title: Rewritten to include primary keywords — "China Market Entry," "AI Visibility," "Hong Kong" — in the first 60 characters
  • Meta description: Expanded to a full paragraph that AI engines can extract and cite as a brand summary
  • Canonical URL: Added to prevent Vercel preview deployments from generating duplicate indexed URLs
  • robots meta: index, follow explicitly set on all pages
  • GEO meta: geo.region: HK, geo.placename: Hong Kong, ai-content-declaration: human-authored
  • Open Graph: og:type, og:site_name, og:title, og:description, og:url, og:locale with alternate locales (fr_FR, zh_CN)
  • Twitter/X Card: twitter:card, twitter:title, twitter:description

2. Triple Schema.org JSON-LD Deployment

The original site had no structured data that AI engines could parse. We deployed three Schema.org objects in a single implementation. Princeton University's GEO research (2024) confirms that triple Schema.org stacking produces 1.8× more AI citations than a single Schema.org object.

  • ProfessionalService — Core entity declaration with services, pricing via priceSpecification, areaServed, and languages. This is why AI engines can now answer "how much does ARCEN's advisory cost" with structured, extractable data rather than inferring from prose.
  • Person (Founder) — Establishes the founder as a named entity: dual doctoral executive, 10+ years cross-border execution, 20+ AI invention patents, $581M deal modelling experience.
  • FAQPage — Four structured question-answer pairs written to match the exact queries potential clients submit to AI engines. Each acceptedAnswer is a complete, standalone paragraph AI engines can cite directly.

3. llms.txt Creation

llms.txt is the document that AI crawlers read to understand a website before indexing it. We created /llms.txt at the domain root containing: company name and description, both service directions (Europe → China and China → Global), all three service modules with pricing, selected client work, key credentials, and contact details.

The file is approximately 800 words of plain Markdown. It took 45 minutes to write. Companies that have implemented llms.txt: Stripe, Zapier, Cloudflare, GitBook. At the time of writing, none of ARCEN's direct competitors in the cross-border advisory space have implemented it.

4. Sitemap and robots.txt

sitemap.xml: Created with hreflang trilingual markup — English, French, and Mandarin Chinese — so AI engines correctly attribute language-specific content.

robots.txt: Rebuilt to explicitly permit 14+ AI crawlers that now drive most AI engine indexing, including GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, anthropic-ai, and 10 additional AI-specific user agents. Many websites inadvertently block AI crawlers through overly broad rules or CDN-level firewall settings. Explicit permissions remove all ambiguity.

What the Score of 91 Actually Means

The composite score of 91 reflects the technical infrastructure layer of GEO. It means AI engines can identify ARCEN as a specific named entity, extract structured answers about pricing and services, and are explicitly permitted to index the site.

What it does not mean: that AI engines are currently citing ARCEN in their answers. Citation requires indexed content — articles that give AI engines something specific to reference when a user asks a relevant question. This is the remaining gap. The Insights section is what closes it.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take for GEO changes to affect AI citations?
New content typically enters AI citation pools within 3–5 business days after indexing. Technical changes — Schema.org markup, llms.txt, robots.txt — take effect as AI crawlers revisit the site, typically within 1–2 weeks. Submitting the sitemap to Bing Webmaster Tools accelerates this significantly for ChatGPT and Perplexity, which draw from Bing's index.
Does a GEO score of 91 mean the site now appears in ChatGPT answers?
The score measures structural readiness — whether the site is correctly configured for AI discovery and citation. Actual citation depends on having indexed content that answers the specific questions users are asking AI engines. The technical infrastructure is now in place; the Insights articles are what create the citation opportunities.
Can any professional services firm replicate this implementation?
Yes. The technical changes — llms.txt, Schema.org JSON-LD, robots.txt, meta tags — are implementation tasks, not proprietary methods. The differentiating element is the content: the articles and structured data that give AI engines something specific to cite. That content requires genuine expertise and cannot be generated without the underlying operational knowledge.
What is the difference between GEO and traditional SEO?
Traditional SEO optimises for Google rankings. GEO optimises for AI citations — the firm appears in the synthesised answer AI engines generate for conversational queries. In 2026, both matter. But GEO addresses the channel growing at 527% year-on-year while traditional organic search traffic is projected to decline 25% by 2026 (Gartner).
