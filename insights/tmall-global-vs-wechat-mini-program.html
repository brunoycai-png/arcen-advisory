Quick Answer: Foreign brands without a Chinese subsidiary have two main e-commerce options: Tmall Global (overseas entity only, USD 25,000 security deposit, 5% commission + USD 5,000–10,000 annual fee, cannot convert to Chinese entity) and WeChat Mini Programs (requires both overseas and Chinese domestic entity, RMB 10,000 security deposit, 0.5% withdrawal fee, can convert to Chinese entity). Tmall Global is better for product discovery; WeChat Mini Programs are better for CRM-driven direct commerce. Neither replaces the structural advantages of a WFOE.

Tmall Global

Alibaba's cross-border e-commerce platform, designed specifically for foreign brands selling into China without a Chinese entity. Products ship from bonded warehouses or directly from overseas to Chinese consumers.

Fee Structure

Entity Change Rules

❌ Overseas entity → Chinese entity: not supported. Once a Tmall Global store is registered under an overseas entity, it cannot later be converted to a Chinese entity. This means the Tmall Global presence becomes a parallel channel in Phase 3 — not the foundation to build on.

Strategic use: Best used as a transitional e-commerce entry point — test product-market fit, build GMV history, and generate revenue while the WFOE is being established. Treat it as a parallel channel, not a foundation.

WeChat Mini Program

A lightweight application running inside WeChat — China's dominant messaging and payment platform with 1.3+ billion monthly active users. Functions as a branded online store accessible directly within WeChat.

Fee Structure

Fee TypeAmount
Security depositRMB 10,000 (~USD 1,460)
Store revenue withdrawal fee0.5% per withdrawal, minimum RMB 100 (~USD 15)

Entity requirement: Registration requires both an overseas entity and a Chinese domestic entity — more complex than Tmall Global, and effectively requires some form of Chinese operational presence.

Entity Change Rules

✅ Overseas entity → Chinese entity: supported. WeChat Mini Programs offer the most structural flexibility — the ability to convert to a WFOE structure preserves the option to migrate without rebuilding the commercial presence.

Strategic use: Most valuable for brands with existing Chinese customer relationships who want to activate direct-to-consumer commerce. The dual entity requirement adds initial complexity, but the structural flexibility makes it the better long-term foundation of the two cross-border options.

Recommended Phase-Based Approach

  • Phase 1: Personal social media accounts for audience building (zero structural commitment)
  • Phase 2: Tmall Global for initial e-commerce revenue + WeChat Mini Program if existing customer base exists
  • Phase 3: WFOE established; all accounts migrated or newly registered under the owned entity; Tmall Global becomes one channel within a broader owned platform ecosystem

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Tmall Global and WeChat Mini Programs

Does Tmall Global require a Chinese trademark?

Tmall Global requires trademark documentation as part of brand verification. A registered Chinese trademark or a pending application with supporting evidence is typically required. Specific requirements vary by product category.

Can a WeChat Mini Program be used for live commerce?

WeChat Mini Programs can integrate with WeChat Video Channels for live streaming, enabling a content-to-commerce flow within the WeChat ecosystem. This is an increasingly important capability as live commerce accounts for a growing share of Chinese e-commerce transactions.

What is the difference between Tmall and Tmall Global?

Tmall (domestic) requires a Chinese entity and serves domestic consumers with domestic logistics. Tmall Global is specifically designed for cross-border commerce under overseas entities, with products shipped from bonded warehouses or from overseas. The two platforms are distinct registrations with different entity requirements and fee structures.

Questions fréquentes

Questions courantes sur Tmall Global et WeChat Mini Programs

Tmall Global nécessite-t-il une marque chinoise ?

Tmall Global exige une documentation de marque dans le cadre de la vérification de marque. Une marque chinoise enregistrée ou une demande en cours avec des preuves à l'appui est généralement requise. Les exigences spécifiques varient selon la catégorie de produits.

Un WeChat Mini Program peut-il être utilisé pour le commerce en direct ?

Les WeChat Mini Programs peuvent s'intégrer aux WeChat Video Channels pour le livestreaming, permettant un flux contenu-vers-commerce dans l'écosystème WeChat.

Quelle est la différence entre Tmall et Tmall Global ?

Tmall (domestique) nécessite une entité chinoise. Tmall Global est spécifiquement conçu pour le commerce transfrontalier sous entités étrangères, avec des produits expédiés depuis des entrepôts sous douane ou depuis l'étranger.

常见问题

关于天猫国际和微信小程序的常见问题

天猫国际需要中国商标吗？

天猫国际在品牌核实过程中要求商标文件。通常需要已注册的中国商标或附有佐证材料的待审申请。具体要求因产品类目而异。

微信小程序可以用于直播带货吗？

微信小程序可以与微信视频号集成进行直播，在微信生态系统内实现内容到商务的流转。随着直播电商在中国电商交易中占比不断增长，这一能力日益重要。

天猫和天猫国际有什么区别？

天猫（国内版）需要中国主体，服务国内消费者，使用国内物流。天猫国际专为境外主体跨境商务设计，产品从保税仓或境外发货。两个平台是不同的注册，具有不同的主体要求和费用结构。
